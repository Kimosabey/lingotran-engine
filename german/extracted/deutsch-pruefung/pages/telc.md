## TELC German Exam Preparation Guide

TELC German exams are CEFR-based certificate exams that assess reading, listening, writing, and speaking through written and oral exam parts. Use the required level from your authority, employer, university, or course provider.

### Official exam facts

- TELC A1 and A2 focus on everyday communication and include written and oral exam parts.
- TELC B1 and B2 include receptive tasks such as reading, listening, and language elements, plus productive writing and speaking tasks.
- TELC states that its certificates are recognized by universities, companies, and state agencies throughout the German-speaking world and internationally.

### Which exam fits your goal?

#### Visa and residence requirements

The requested TELC certificate and level can be relevant for visa and residence contexts. Match the exact certificate name requested by the authority.

#### Citizenship and official procedures

If your authority asks for B1 German, TELC B1 is a relevant preparation target because it tests everyday communication across all skills.

#### Work and professional communication

B1 supports everyday workplace communication. B2 is better for independent professional language, clearer argumentation, and more complex input.

#### University and higher education

TELC certificates may be relevant for higher education, but many German-taught degree programs ask for C1-level or specific proofs such as telc Deutsch C1 Hochschule.

### Which level should you practise?

#### A1 - Simple everyday German

Beginner reading, listening, writing, and speaking for basic everyday communication.

#### A2 - Routine communication

Familiar topics, common expressions, simple writing prompts, and oral interaction with another test taker and examiner.

#### B1 - Personal and professional basics

Everyday communication in personal and professional situations, with both receptive and productive task types.

#### B2 - Advanced everyday communication

Upper-intermediate reading, listening, language elements, writing, and paired speaking.

#### C1 - Advanced academic and professional German

Demanding reading texts, dense Sprachbausteine, radio-style listening, argumentative writing, and nuanced oral expression.
