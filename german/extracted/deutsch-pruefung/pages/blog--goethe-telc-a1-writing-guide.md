## Mastering the Goethe & TELC A1 Writing Section

The writing task in the Goethe and TELC A1 exams proves you can communicate in everyday German. You'll complete a short functional task and compose a mini letter or email. With a clear structure, simple vocabulary, and the right greetings and endings, you can earn easy points and finish confidently.

2 focused tasks

Complete a form or note and write a short message (around 40–50 words).

25–30 minutes total

Goethe A1: 20 minutes · TELC A1: 30 minutes for writing tasks.

Simple German works

Clear sentences, correct word order, and polite tone win more points than fancy words.

## How the Writing Section Is Structured

### Task 1 · Forms or Short Notes (5–10 minutes)

- Fill in personal information (name, address, phone) or write a short message.
- Focus on spelling and capital letters for nouns.
- Answer exactly what the prompt asks—no extra sentences needed.

Sample form entry

Name: Priya Sharma

Geburtsdatum: 12.03.1996

Adresse: Hauptstraße 12, 70173 Stuttgart

Telefon: 0151 23456789

### Task 2 · Short Text or Letter (15–20 minutes)

- Write 4–6 sentences (about 40–60 words) answering every bullet in the prompt.
- Use present tense, simple past (Perfekt), and future (using werden) sparingly if requested.
- Structure your answer with greeting, body (3–4 points), and a polite closing.

Quick outline

1. Greeting (formal or informal)
2. Purpose sentence
3. Answer each bullet (1 sentence each)
4. Friendly farewell + signature

## Proven Strategies for High Scores

### Smart planning

- Underline key instructions: name, date, place, invitation, response, etc.
- Decide on formal vs informal tone before you start writing.
- Draft sentence starters in the margin, then copy neatly to the answer sheet.

### Language essentials

- Keep verbs in second position: Ich komme morgen.
- Use capital letters for nouns: die Party, die Einladung.
- Connect ideas with simple words: und, aber, denn, oder, deshalb, weil.
- Include at least one question in informal letters to keep the conversation natural.

## Choosing the Right Tone: Informal vs Formal

![Example cue cards for Goethe and TELC A1 writing section](https://deutsch-pruefung.de/_next/image?url=%2Fblog%2Fimages%2Fblog-image3.jpg&w=3840&q=75)

### Informal Letters

Used for friends, family, classmates, or roommates.

Openings

- Hallo [Name],
- Liebe [Name],
- Lieber [Name],

Closings

- Liebe Grüße,
- Bis bald!
- Viele Grüße,

Sample sentence starters

Ich hoffe, es geht dir gut.

Danke für deine Nachricht.

Kommst du morgen zur Party?

### Formal Letters

Use for offices, schools, landlords, course coordinators, or unknown recipients.

- Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,
- Sehr geehrter Herr [Name],
- Sehr geehrte Frau [Name],

- Mit freundlichen Grüßen,
- Hochachtungsvoll,
- Vielen Dank und freundliche Grüße,

Ich schreibe Ihnen, weil...

Ich möchte mich nach dem Deutschkurs erkundigen.

Vielen Dank für Ihre Hilfe.

## Common Mistakes and Quick Fixes

### Watch out for

- Skipping one of the bullet points in the task.
- Mixing informal sentences with formal greetings.
- Forgetting capital letters for nouns.
- Verb in the wrong position: *Ich morgen komme* ❌

### Quick fixes

- Tick off each bullet after you write the sentence.
- Keep a list of polite phrases and learn the correct endings (du vs Sie).
- Read your text aloud quietly. If it sounds natural, it probably is.
- Check verbs last: subject + conjugated verb + rest.

## Exam-Day Writing Checklist

- Read the full task twice and underline key words.
- Spend one minute planning your 3–4 body sentences.
- Use connectors to join ideas but keep each sentence under 12 words.
- Include one nice detail (time, place, reason) to sound natural.
- Always finish with a closing phrase and your name.
- Reserve 2 minutes to proofread spelling, verbs, and punctuation.
